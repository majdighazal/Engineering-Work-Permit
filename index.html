Contractors Safety Regulations
The contractor and all contractor personnel must comply with the following safety regulations throughout the work.
  1. Contractors will ensure that their personnel are provided with appropriate safety equipment for the work being carried out.
  2. Work areas will be cordoned off with suitable barriers where necessary. Barriers must prevent unauthorised access to the work area. Danger tape is not permitted for cordoning off work areas.
  3. Appropriate warning signs will be displayed next to the cordoned-off work area.
  4. Contractors will ensure that their personnel are briefed on the safe use of all tools and equipment required to carry out the work.
  5. Contractors will ensure that all tools and equipment in use are safe and fit for purpose.
  6. Any equipment requiring certification, including lifting equipment and air-compressor vessels, must have current valid certification.
  7. All electric power tools must be fitted with appropriate guards and electrical plugs.
  8. Electrical extension leads must be fitted with appropriate plugs and be free from cuts and kinks.
  9. Before work begins, contractors must provide a list and all required Safety Data Sheets (SDS/MSDS) for hazardous or toxic substances brought onto the site. Such substances must be stored in accordance with good practice.
  10. Smoking and eating on site are permitted only in the designated areas.
  11. The work area must be kept clean and tidy at all times. Fire and safety hazards, including oily rags and flammable materials, must be removed promptly. The contractor is responsible for removing all waste materials from the site.
  12. When working in or around swimming pools, Conrad will provide lifeguard cover. No work may be carried out without a lifeguard present.
  13. When using a ladder, the contractor must ensure that it is in good condition and that a second person secures the bottom of the ladder at all times while it is in use.
  14. IMPORTANT! The team member signing below must ensure that all relevant systems & equipment are locked out in accordance with policy & guidelines, and that all supplementary permits have been issued.
